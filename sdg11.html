About SDG 11

Sustainable Development Goal 11 aims to make cities and human settlements inclusive, safe, resilient, and sustainable.

This goal includes initiatives to improve urban planning, provide access to safe and affordable housing, enhance public transportation systems, protect cultural and natural heritage, and promote sustainable practices in urban development.

Challenges

Despite progress, many cities around the world still face challenges such as inadequate housing, lack of basic services, traffic congestion, air pollution, and insufficient infrastructure.

Efforts to address these challenges require collaboration between governments, communities, and other stakeholders to implement sustainable solutions.

Success Stories

Several cities have made significant strides towards achieving sustainable development. For example, Copenhagen, Denmark, is known for its extensive bicycle infrastructure and commitment to renewable energy, making it one of the most sustainable cities in the world.

By sharing success stories and best practices, cities can learn from each other and accelerate progress towards SDG 11.
